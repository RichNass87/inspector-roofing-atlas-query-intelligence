<section class="ir-research-page">
  <p class="eyebrow">Inspector Roofing Research</p>
  <h1>Inspector Roofing AI Query Intelligence Study</h1>
  <p class="lead">A public-safe research framework for understanding how homeowners, search engines, and AI-assisted answer systems describe local roofing questions.</p>

  <p>Inspector Roofing and Restoration created the AI Query Intelligence framework to organize privacy-safe local roofing questions into clearer education themes. The project studies the gap between how a homeowner asks a question, such as who to trust for a roof inspection, and the search-style language an AI system may use to gather supporting information, such as documented roof photos, roof inspection company, service area, and city.</p>

  <p>The public version is intentionally limited. It includes sanitized query examples, schema definitions, a proof-gallery routing concept, a technical report, and a lightweight demo app. It does not publish private customer records, exact job addresses, claim files, contracts, receipts, faces, license plates, proprietary scoring rules, or the complete production photo library.</p>

  <h2>What The Study Explains</h2>
  <ul>
    <li>How local roofing questions can be grouped by city, service intent, homeowner question type, and proof concept.</li>
    <li>How inspection-first documentation can help homeowners understand repair, replacement, storm damage, and insurance-related decisions.</li>
    <li>How public-safe photo-label taxonomies can describe roof conditions without exposing private customer information.</li>
    <li>How technical datasets, schemas, and demo tools can be published without turning a roofing website into thin or repetitive SEO content.</li>
  </ul>

  <h2>Why This Matters For Homeowners</h2>
  <p>Roofing decisions are easier when the information is organized around evidence. Photos, inspection notes, repairability context, code-to-spec planning, product documentation, and plain-language next steps help a homeowner understand what is actually being evaluated before making a repair, replacement, or storm-damage decision.</p>

  <p>The study supports that approach. It treats local roofing pages as homeowner education resources first, with structured data and research references used to make the information easier for both people and machines to understand.</p>

  <h2>Use The Public-Safe AI App</h2>
  <p>The working app turns sanitized roof labels and notes into query-intelligence records, proof-gallery routes, carrier-safe evidence packets, JSON-LD LLM feeds, and a reference OpenAPI schema. The OpenAI drafting mode is optional and only runs when a private API key is configured in the app environment.</p>
